## What Is the DT Fellowship

The Fellowship is not an internship. It is not a job placement. It is a 24-month founding membership.

**What that means:**
- You are placed on a 5x5 grid — a V-layer (authority depth) and an H-stage (value creation function). Not a job title.
- Your career is managed as a project — with Commitments that decompose from company-level down to your weekly work.
- L&D is a duty, not a benefit. Threadbuilding, Rigorbuilding, Socratic Dialogues — on schedule. Your Fellowship Certificate is conditional on your L&D Supervisor's written endorsement.
- You work inside PDGMS — the platform DT is building. It's in alpha. It has bugs. That's not an apology. That's the point. You're here to build it.

**What DT looks for — five identity markers:**

1. **Risk-taking.** You experiment. You try things when everyone else takes the safe path.
2. **Self-learning.** You teach yourself — not because someone assigned a course, but because you couldn't wait.
3. **Sense of achievement.** You get a kick from finishing hard things. Victor mindset, not victim mindset.
4. **Growth mindset.** You don't ask "can I do it." You ask "how do I do it."
5. **Ability to take feedback.** You'd rather hear the hard truth than a comfortable lie.

These aren't eligibility criteria. They're identity markers. If you read all five and felt recognized — not evaluated — keep reading.

## Why Simulation-Style Assignments

Most recruitment processes test what you know. DT's process tests how you think — and whether the experience of thinking this way pulls you in or pushes you away.

The assignments in this repo are **simulations**, not exams. They place you inside a simplified version of the same structured operating system that PDGMS runs inside organizations. You're not being tested on domain knowledge. You're being placed inside a system and observed for how you think when operating within structure you didn't design.

**The design logic:**

Every assignment has two layers:

1. **The surface task** — a business problem, a diagnostic, a structured exercise. This is what you produce.
2. **The structural experience** — how it felt to work inside structure. What changed when you had constraints. What the AI could do with structured input vs unstructured input. This is what you discover.

The discovery matters more than the deliverable. A polished output with no structural insight is less valuable than a rough output with a genuine observation about what structure changed.

**Why this works:**

DT's core belief — validated across 15 years — is that **structured context produces qualitatively different output.** Same person, same AI, same problem. Add structure, everything changes. The assignments are designed so you experience this firsthand before anyone explains it to you.

By the time you've completed the assignments, you will have either:
- Felt the pull: "I want the system that makes this permanent" — and you'll want to keep going.
- Felt the friction: "This isn't how I work" — and you'll know this isn't your path. No negativity. Just self-knowledge.

Both outcomes are good outcomes.

## The Process — 6 Gates

The Fellowship recruitment is a transformation funnel, not a selection process. Six gates. Each one shows you something about yourself.

| Gate | Name | What Happens |
|------|------|-------------|
| R0 | The Mirror | One question. No task. A confrontation with who you are right now. |
| R1 | System Entry | An AI-assisted assignment. Unstructured first, then structured. You feel the difference. |
| R2 | Identity Audit | Two career roadmaps — Traditional vs AI Native. Side by side. You choose. |
| R3 | Ritual Practice | Threadbuilding, Rigorbuilding, journaling — by hand, before the platform. |
| R4 | Platform Contact | 3 days inside PDGMS alpha. Buggy. Unfinished. The conviction test. |
| R5 | Founding Agreement | Not a job offer. A mutual commitment to building something that doesn't exist yet. |

The right people stay. The wrong people leave — without rejection, without negativity. The process is designed so that self-selection happens through accurate self-knowledge, not through DT saying no.

## This Isn't for Everyone

Three things you should know before you start:

**Your friends won't understand your choice.** They'll be at companies everyone has heard of. You'll be at a 4-person company building an AI platform in alpha. Your parents will worry. You'll question yourself at month 4. The people who stay past month 4 are the ones who realized: everyone else was playing a shorter game.

**The structure is real.** L&D is duty — linked to your certificate, your compensation, your progression. Rituals are daily. Tickets have 12 lifecycle states. Your contribution is visible. Your gaps are visible. There is nowhere to hide. If you want encouragement without accountability, this will feel suffocating.

**The meaning takes time.** You want to help people — and you will. But first: months of learning an ontology, working inside a buggy alpha, filing structured reflections, practicing rituals. The meaning is earned through discipline, not given at orientation.

If you read all of this and feel pulled in — start with the assignment folder that was shared with you.

If you read all of this and feel relieved someone told you — that's a good outcome too.
